DAY 01

Delhi → Amritsar

450 km 7–9 hrs NH44 via Panipat, Ludhiana

Visit the Golden Temple at dusk — worth every minute. Optional Wagah Border ceremony if you arrive in time.

Amritsar → Srinagar

430 km 10–12 hrs Pathankot → Jammu → Ramban → Banihal

The longest day. Heavy traffic near Ramban — start before 6 AM. Tunnel through Banihal cuts time significantly.

DAY 03

Srinagar → Kargil

200 km 7–9 hrs Sonamarg → Zoji La → Drass

First taste of the high Himalayas. Zoji La pass demands respect — start early and ride steady. Drass is the second coldest inhabited place on Earth.

DAY 04

Kargil → Leh

220 km 6–8 hrs

A landmark day. Pass Lamayuru Monastery, the Moonland landscape, and the famous Magnetic Hill before rolling into Leh.

Scenic Ride

DAY 05

Leh — Rest & Explore

No ride Altitude: ~3500m

Mandatory acclimatization day. Visit Shanti Stupa at sunrise, Leh Palace, and the local market. Hydrate and take it slow — your body is adjusting.

DAY 06

Leh → Turtuk

200 km 7–9 hrs via Khardung La → Nubra Valley

Khardung La at 5359m — one of the world's highest motorable roads. Nubra Valley opens like a revelation. Turtuk, a Balti village, is otherworldly.

DAY 07

Turtuk → Siachen Base Camp → Diskit/Hunder

220–260 km 8–10 hrs Partapur → Thoise → SBC

Rare access territory. Carry all permits and IDs — multiple checkpoints. Road beyond Thoise can be rough. Return to Nubra for the night.

DAY 08

Nubra → Pangong Lake

180–220 km 7–9 hrs Diskit → Shyok → Pangong

Remote Shyok Valley route — stunning but unpaved in stretches. Check road conditions the evening before. The first sight of Pangong's blue is breathtaking.

DAY 09

Hanle → Umling La → Hanle ★

150 km 6–8 hrs 5798m altitude

The crown jewel. World's highest motorable pass. Extreme altitude, thin air — ride slowly, breathe deeply. This is why you came.

DAY 10

Hanle → Tso Moriri

220 km 7–8 hrs

A remote, meditative ride through the Changthang plateau. Tso Moriri is quieter and less visited than Pangong — camp here if you can.

DAY 11

Tso Moriri → Sarchu / Jispa

250 km 8–10 hrs

Long but manageable. Jispa is the safer, more comfortable stop. Sarchu is at high altitude — acclimatization issues sometimes arise overnight there.

Sarchu / Jispa → Manali

140 km 6–7 hrs via Baralacha La & Rohtang

The descent from the Himalayas. Rohtang can be congested — check permit requirements in advance. Manali feels like civilization after Ladakh.

DAY 13

Manali → Delhi

500 km 10–12 hrs

The final stretch. NH3 to NH44 — straightforward highway riding. The miles will feel long, but the memories will last a lifetime.

Final Ride
Final Ride
01

Acclimatize, Don't Rush

Altitude sickness is real and dangerous. Plan two full rest days in Leh before pushing higher. Carry Diamox and know the symptoms.

02

Permits in Advance

Inner Line Permits for Turtuk, Siachen, Pangong, and Hanle are mandatory. Apply at least 2 weeks ahead. Carry photocopies everywhere.

03

Fuel Discipline

Fill up at every opportunity. Fuel stations are sparse beyond Leh — always carry at least 5L extra. Petrol quality can vary; carry octane booster.

Essential Checklist

🏍️

Riding Gear

  • Full-face helmet (DOT/ISI rated)
  • Riding jacket with CE armour
  • Waterproof riding gloves
  • Ankle-protection boots
  • Riding pants with knee guards
  • Thermal underlayer
  • Rain suit / waterproof cover
📋

Documents

  • Driving Licence (original + copy)
  • Vehicle RC + Insurance
  • Inner Line Permits (multiple)
  • Government Photo ID (Aadhar)
  • Emergency contacts list
  • Hotel confirmations printed
🔧

Bike & Tools

  • Full service before departure
  • Basic toolkit (spanners, pliers)
  • Puncture repair kit + inflator
  • 5L reserve fuel canister
  • Clutch & brake levers (spares)
  • Zip ties, duct tape, wire
  • Engine oil (1L extra)
💊

Health & Safety

  • Diamox (altitude sickness)
  • Dolo 650 / paracetamol
  • First aid kit
  • Oral rehydration salts (ORS)
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+
  • Lip balm & moisturiser
  • Hydration bladder / bottles
📡

Navigation & Comms

  • Offline maps downloaded (Maps.me)
  • BSNL SIM (best Ladakh coverage)
  • Portable power bank (20000mAh)
  • Phone mount (vibration-damped)
  • Walkie-talkies (for groups)
🎒

Clothing & Camp

  • Fleece jacket + down jacket
  • Woollen socks (4–5 pairs)
  • Quick-dry t-shirts & trousers
  • Balaclava & neck gaiter
  • Sunglasses (UV400)
  • Sleeping bag (rated -10°C)
